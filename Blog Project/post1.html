Relationship Equality

Let's discuss relationship equality. Not so much in a sense of who takes care of what, pays for what, cleans, cooks, etc. More so in a sense of respectfulness on common grounds. So what I’m getting at is when "John" and "Sara" are in a disagreement or not seeing eye to eye on something, Sara's response to any given situation is challenged with the thought that she's always making excuses. Anything she says is an excuse for something versus an actual fact that pertains to the subject at matter. For example, if intercourse is the topic and not enough of it is happening for whatever reason, if she say's "I'm up cleaning before the kids wake, busy with them back to back until they’re ready for bed, and John is in bed sleeping before the kids are, where is the opportunity for intercourse if I have to wake him up to do so?" She knows she can if he is fine with that but the fact that he is sleeping and felt the need to do so before the rest of the family just shows that he is not interested in waiting for a moment to have alone with Sara. Mind you, she's just had a busy day and is tired too but it’s 12am and she hasn’t even showered yet. According to John, that is all considered an excuse versus it being actual facts of what is going on. Now, irritation and anger is involved and the pressure is put on Sara to fix it because regardless of how her day goes with the kids or how busy she can be, if she is not initiating action or anything to insinuate that she wants to have intercourse then it’s just left with the thought that she doesn’t try hard enough. Why does Sara have to deal with initiating or fixing the issues but John just gets to point the finger?
